Project Overview

MetLife is a leading global insurance provider with nearly 43,000 employees globally. The Innovation Challenge at MetLife is a challenge for interns and program attendees to collaboratively devise and pitch creative solutions to targeted challenges. Our goal this year was to "reimagine onboarding to ensure a modern high-touch and high-tech experience that accelerates and fosters connection, inclusion, and productivity."

MetSetGo addresses the challenges of decentralization, resource navigation, and complexity in scheduling. MetSetGo is a three-component, self-service, employee onboarding system that focuses on a new hire's first five days of onboarding. MetSetGo aims to leverage technology to mitigate potential opportunities for miscommunication between hiring managers and recently employed staff. The system standardizes learning and training for each role to ensure there is no knowledge gap. Resources also become more accessible, as MetSetGo would serve as an all-encompassing platform when searching for employee-related information.

What I learned

I learned from this project how to take calculated risks. Moreover, I should not fear risk, I should learn to weigh my options thoroughly before acting on them to meet deadlines and goals while achieving what I want.

What I'd do differently

In redoing this project, I would be bold in asking for more clarity on the parameters of this project. I would also conduct more reviews with the stakeholders to gauge what they genuinely want. Internally, I would conduct more structured meetings with smaller targets while providing more role clarity.
